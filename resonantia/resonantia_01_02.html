II. Corridor

Elaine left the hall. Sound returned. Footsteps. Cloth brushing cloth. Soles against stone. The corridor stretched long and straight before her. Light from outside slipped in, laying a pale band across the floor. As she walked, she steadied her breath. The rhythm had finally returned, yet it still didn’t feel completely hers. Her steps were steady, but the floor seemed to shift faintly beneath her with every one. Somewhere deep inside, water still lingered. She didn’t know what it was. “Lieutenant Aldia.” A voice called out. Curtis Harwood fell into step beside her, matching her pace without effort. Deputy commander of the mage corps. They had met once before her assignment. “No issues?” he asked briefly. Elaine drew a single breath and answered. “Yes.” Curtis nodded. Behind his glasses, his gaze softened. They continued down the corridor. The light from outside grew a little stronger. “…As scheduled,” he said. His eyes flicked toward her for a moment, then returned forward. “After this, there will be formalities with the corps.” Elaine nodded. “How was the hall?” Curtis asked lightly. She paused, just slightly. The ritual hall—a place she had often visited as a child. “…It was quiet.” Curtis let out a small, quiet laugh. “Yes.” They kept walking. Ahead, a single figure came into view. A tall man in deep navy uniform, standing perfectly still. The moment she saw him, something inside her shifted. Their eyes hadn’t even met yet, but something already passed between them—wordless and deep. He was already looking at her. Curtis slowed, falling a step behind. The man approached with unwavering footsteps and stopped at a measured distance. “Captain Valen,” Curtis said first. The man gave a slight nod. His gaze settled on Elaine. Hawthorn-brown eyes. “This is Lieutenant Elaine Aldia,” Curtis introduced. “Lance Valen,” the man replied shortly. Silence. Elaine gave the smallest nod. Lance’s gaze remained on her. His expression didn’t change. Then it lowered. “That is all.” Curtis stepped forward again. “Then we’ll proceed to the formalities.” His voice restored the flow. Lance said nothing more. Elaine glanced at him once more as she passed. He remained exactly where he had stood. Behind her, his footsteps did not follow.
