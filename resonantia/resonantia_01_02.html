II. Corridor

Elaine leaves the chapel. Sound returns. Footsteps. Cloth brushing. The feel of soles against stone. The corridor stretches long, in a straight line. Light from outside slips in, forming a pale band across the floor. As she walks, she steadies her breathing. The delayed rhythm has already aligned. And yet, she cannot say it has fully returned. Her footing is certain. But with each step, the distance to the floor does not quite settle. Somewhere deep within, the presence of water remains. She does not know what it is. “Lieutenant Aldia.” A voice calls. Curtis Harwood steps in beside her, matching her pace. Deputy commander of the mage corps— they had met before her assignment. His movement is quiet, without strain. “No issues?” A brief check. Elaine takes a breath once, and answers. “Yes.” Curtis nods. Behind his glasses, his gaze softens. They proceed along the corridor. The light from outside grows slightly stronger. “…As scheduled.” For a moment, his gaze turns to her. Then returns. “After this, there will be formalities with the corps.” Elaine nods. “How was the chapel?” Curtis asks, lightly. Elaine pauses, just a little. The chapel. A place she often visited as a child. “…It was quiet.” Curtis lets out a small laugh. “Yes.” They continue walking. Ahead, a single figure comes into view. A deep navy uniform. A tall man. Standing, precisely, in place. The moment she sees him, something within her responds. Before their eyes meet, there is already contact. It does not become words. Only—deep. The man is already looking at her. Curtis slows, taking a step back. The man approaches. His footsteps do not waver. He comes directly toward them. He stops at a measured distance. “Captain Valen.” Curtis speaks first. The man gives a slight nod. His gaze turns to Elaine. The same eyes as before. Hawthorn-brown. “This is Lieutenant Elaine Aldia.” Curtis says. The man responds, briefly. “Lance Valen.” Silence. Elaine gives the smallest nod. Lance’s gaze remains there. Without changing expression, he lowers his eyes. “That is all.” He says, shortly. At that cue, Curtis steps forward. “Then we’ll proceed to the formalities.” A voice returning the flow. Lance says nothing further. Elaine looks at him once more. He stands in the same place as before. After confirming that, she begins to walk. Behind her, his footsteps do not move.
