II. Corridor

Elaine leaves the hall. Sound returns. Footsteps. Cloth against cloth. Soles on stone. The corridor stretches long, in a straight line. Light from outside slips in, forming a pale band across the floor. As she walks, she steadies her breath. The rhythm is finally in step, but it does not feel completely restored. Her footing was steady, but with every step, the floor seemed to shift beneath her. Somewhere deep within, water remains. She does not know what it is. “Lieutenant Aldia.” A voice calls out. Curtis Harwood steps in beside her, matching her pace. Deputy commander of the mage corps— they had met before her assignment. His movement is quiet, without strain. “No issues?” He checks, briefly. Elaine takes a breath once, and answers. “Yes.” Curtis nods. Behind his glasses, his gaze softens. They proceed along the corridor. The light from outside grows slightly stronger. “…As scheduled.” For a moment, his gaze turns to her, then returns. “After this, there will be formalities with the corps.” Elaine nods. “How was the hall?” Curtis asks, lightly. Elaine pauses, just a little. The ritual hall. A place she often visited as a child. “…It was quiet.” Curtis lets out a small laugh. “Yes.” They continue walking. Ahead, a single figure comes into view. A tall man in a deep navy uniform, Standing unmoving. The moment she sees him, something within her shifts. Before their eyes meet, there is already touching It does not become words. Only—deep. The man is already looking at her. Curtis slows, taking a step back. The man approaches, his footsteps unwavering, straight toward them, then stops at a measured distance. “Captain Valen.” Curtis speaks first. The man gives a slight nod. His gaze falls on Elaine, those same eyes from before. Hawthorn-brown. “This is Lieutenant Elaine Aldia.” Curtis says. The man responds, briefly. “Lance Valen.” Silence. Elaine gives the smallest nod. Lance’s gaze remains there. His expression does not change, his gaze lowers. “That is all.” He says, shortly. Curtis steps forward. “Then we’ll proceed to the formalities.” The voice restores the flow. Lance says nothing further. Elaine looks at him once more. He stands in the same place as before. After a brief glance, she moves on. Behind her, his footsteps do not move.
