I. The Ritual Chapel

— Calnaria, the walled city. The boundary is quietly maintained. Those who sustain it are the mages. *** On that day, Elaine Aldia stood on the verge of her assignment to the Capital Mage Corps. All that remained was the rite in the chapel. Once it was complete, she would formally become a member of the corps. Passing through a stone corridor, the space opened. The ceiling was high, light seeping in from deep within the walls. Stone, almost white, returned sound in a softened way. At the center lay a shallow circular basin. Along its rim, fine engraved lines ran in layered rings. It was old, but well kept. Water was brought. A porcelain vessel, white. Not much of it. A priest in white robes stood beside the basin. Others waited at a slight distance. The mages in military uniform were placed further out. Keeping space between one another, they stood tracing a circle. All of them were men. Among them, only Elaine differed. Elaine took her assigned position. The priest lifted the vessel. Water was poured, quietly. The sound of it touching stone was small. Yet it spread through the entire space. A faint ripple rose on the surface. — It did not settle at once. At the bottom of the basin, the motion did not still. Rather, it seemed to be drawn downward. Sound receded. The outline of the space loosened, slightly. Elaine’s breath fell a beat behind. No one moved. The mages along the outer ring shifted only their gaze, slightly. Not enough yet to be called abnormal. But the balance was beginning to falter. The surface sank, deeply. As though the basin, which should have had a bottom, continued further inward. Then, at one point along the outer ring, someone moved. A gloved hand rose, just slightly. The angle of the fingers changed. That was all. A thin line passed through the space. Invisible, yet clearly drawn. The sinking surface halted there. Sound returned. The stone walls. The weight of the air. The certainty beneath one’s feet. Everything settled back into its place. No one spoke. One of the mages along the outer ring gave a small nod. Elaine slowly steadied her breath. Ahead of her gaze stood the mage in uniform from before. The man had already lowered his hand. Standing quietly, as if he had done nothing. Only his gaze remained, directed toward her. A brief crossing. The next moment, he turned his eyes away. The water lay there, still and unchanged.
