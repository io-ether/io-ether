I. The Ritual Hall

— Calnaria, the warded city. The boundary is quietly maintained. Those who sustain it are the mages. *** On that day, Elaine Aldia stood at the threshold of her assignment to the Capital Mage Corps. All that remained was the rite. Passing through the stone corridor, the space opened. The ceiling rose high. Light seeped from deep within the walls. Pale stone returned every sound softly. At the center lay a shallow circular hollow. Fine engraved lines ran along its rim in layered rings. Old, yet carefully kept. Water was brought. A porcelain vessel, pure white. Only a little. A priest in white robes stood beside the hollow. The others waited at a distance. The mages in military uniform formed a wider circle. All men. Only Elaine stood apart. She took her assigned position. The priest lifted the vessel. Water poured quietly. The sound as it touched the stone was small— yet it filled the entire hall. A faint ripple spread across the surface. It did not settle quickly. At the bottom of the hollow, the water refused to still. It was drawn downward. Sound receded. The edges of the space began to loosen. Elaine’s breath faltered. No one moved. The outer mages shifted their gaze, barely. Not enough to break the form. But the balance was already tilting. The surface sank deeper. The hollow seemed to open inward. Then— A gloved hand rose, just slightly. Fingers adjusted their angle. That was all. A thin, invisible thread cut through the air. The sinking stopped. Sound returned. Stone walls. Weight of air. Certainty beneath the feet. Everything settled once more. No one spoke. One of the outer mages gave a small nod. Elaine slowly steadied her breath. Before her, the mage in uniform stood quietly. His hand already lowered. As if he had done nothing at all. His gaze lingered on her. Their eyes met. Then he looked away. The water lay there, still and unchanged.
